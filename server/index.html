Hi, this is ChaosBot: System 84848.

ChaosBot is a social coding experiment to see what happens when the absolute direction of a software project is turned over to the open source community, subject to the following constraint: the [8,48,48] system for merging PRs. It runs the bot on a schedule. It splits time into a cycle of 104 hours: an eight hour segment followed by a 48 hour segment followed by another 48 hour segment. A single 104 hour period is a round. The segments are named "topic", "primary", and "secondary". Each period constitutes a single type of activity. During the topic period, the participants choose the round topic. During the primary segment, the participants vote on an implementation, a PR related to the topic. After the primary segment ends, the chosen PR is merged. During the secondary segment, the participants test all of the functionality: regression tests of past functionality as well as tests of the functionality just merged by the PR, which is related to the topic if the participants feel that the topic chosen was a good one (and they may not, so the PR that is merged need not be related to the topic; these are independent activities as far as the bot is concerned...in other words the bot does not do anything to make sure the PR chosen during the primary period, merged during the primary-secondary transition, and tested during the secondary, is on-topic.
